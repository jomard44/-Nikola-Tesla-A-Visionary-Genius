About Nikola Tesla

Tesla was born to Serbian parents in the village of Smiljan, Austrian Empire near the town of Gospić, in the territory of modern-day Croatia. His baptismal certificate reports that he was born on June 28, 1856, to Father Milutin Tesla, a priest in the Serbian Orthodox Church, Metropolitanate of Sremski Karlovci and Đuka Mandić. His paternal origin is thought to be either of one of the local Serb clans in the Tara valley, or from the Herzegovinian noble Pavle Orlović. His mother, Đuka, daughter of a Serbian Orthodox Church priest, came from a family domiciled in Lika and Banija, but with deeper origins to Kosovo. She was talented in making home craft tools and memorized many Serbian epic poems, but never learned to read. Nikola was the fourth of five children, having one older brother (Dane, who was killed in a horse-riding accident when Nikola was five) and three sisters (Milka, Angelina and Marica). His family moved to Gospić in 1862. Tesla went to school in Karlovac. He finished a four-year term in the span of three years.

Timeline

1856 - Nikola Tesla Is Born: Tesla was born to Serbian parents in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). He was the fourth of five children and showed remarkable intelligence from a young age.
1875 - Tesla Enrolls At Austrian Polytechnic: He began his studies in mechanical and electrical engineering and became intrigued by the possibilities of alternating current (AC) after observing a Gramme dynamo.
1882 - Tesla Begins Working for Continental Edison: He moved to Paris and designed improvements to electric equipment based on Edison's ideas. In the same year, he conceived the induction motor and began developing devices using rotating magnetic fields.
1884 - Tesla Arrives in the United States: Tesla immigrated to the United States, working for Thomas Edison's Edison Machine Works, where he focused on solving complex electrical problems.
1885 - Tesla Quits Working for Edison: He left Edison's company after a dispute over promised payments and began focusing on his AC polyphase system.
1886 - Tesla Forms Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company: Tesla founded his own company but faced disagreements with financial investors about the direction of the business.
1887 - War of Currents Era Begins: A battle between Tesla's alternating current (AC) and Edison's direct current (DC) systems began, with Tesla advocating for the use of AC.
1887 - Tesla Experiments with X-Rays: In April, Tesla began experimenting with X-rays using his own vacuum tubes.
1888 - Tesla Delivers Lecture on Alternating Current Motors and Transformers: He presented a lecture introducing the concept of efficient generation and use of polyphase alternating currents.
1891 - Tesla Patents the Tesla Coil: Tesla patented the Tesla coil, which became a key component in generating high voltage, low current, high-frequency electricity.
1891 - Tesla Becomes a U.S. Citizen: Tesla became a naturalized U.S. citizen and established his laboratory in New York, where he conducted groundbreaking experiments.
1893 - Tesla and Westinghouse Power the World's Columbian Exposition: Tesla and George Westinghouse used AC power to illuminate the exposition in Chicago, demonstrating the advantages of alternating current.
1899 - Tesla Moves to Colorado Springs: Tesla conducted high-voltage, high-frequency experiments in Colorado Springs, making significant discoveries in resonance and wireless power transmission.
1901 - Construction Begins on Tesla's Wardenclyffe Tower: Tesla started building the Wardenclyffe Tower in New York to demonstrate wireless power transmission.
1913 - Tesla Patents Bladeless Turbine: Tesla patented a bladeless turbine that could be used for various purposes, including fluid propulsion.
1915 - Tesla and Edison Ignored by Nobel Prize Committee: Tesla and Edison were passed over for the Nobel Prize in Physics in favor of other recipients.
1915 - Tesla Files Lawsuit Against Guglielmo Marconi: Tesla sued Marconi for patent infringement related to radio technology.
1934 - The New York Times Publicizes Tesla's Particle Beam Research: Tesla's work on the "teleforce" particle beam was reported in The New York Times.
1943 - Nikola Tesla Dies: Tesla passed away in New York City, leaving behind a significant scientific legacy.
1960 - 'Tesla' Defined as a Unit of Measure: The tesla, the SI unit of magnetic field strength, was named in honor of Nikola Tesla.

Invantions

AC (Alternating Current): Tesla's advocacy and development of the alternating current (AC) electrical system allowed for efficient long-distance power transmission.
Tesla Coil: The Tesla coil is a resonant transformer used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high-frequency electricity.
Induction Motor: Tesla's design for the induction motor is a fundamental component in modern electrical systems.
X-Rays: Tesla made significant contributions to the early experimentation with X-rays.
Bladeless Turbine: Tesla patented a bladeless turbine that had potential applications in various fields.
Wardenclyffe Tower: While not completed, the Wardenclyffe Tower was intended to demonstrate the concept of wireless power transmission.
Teleforce: Tesla's research on the "teleforce" particle beam projector was groundbreaking.
Nikola Tesla Unit: The tesla (T) was named in honor of Tesla and is the unit of magnetic field strength in the International System of Units (SI).
